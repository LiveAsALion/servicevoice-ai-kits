PHX Pool Pro — Partner Close Call Script

Company: 3A Pools  |  Contact: sales@3apools.com  |  Caller: Alex Rivera  |  Goal: Confirm as Partner #2

Opening

"Hi, is this [name]? Hey, this is Alex Rivera — I reached out a couple weeks ago about the PHX Pool Pro partner program, and you replied saying you were interested. I wanted to give you a quick call to answer any questions and see if it makes sense to get you set up. Do you have about five minutes?"

If they don't remember the email:

"Totally fine — the short version is: I run a lead matching service for Phoenix homeowners looking for pool service. You'd be one of our vetted partners, and I send you qualified leads when a homeowner submits a request. No upfront cost, $99 per lead only when I send one."

Early Close Attempt

"Based on your reply, it sounded like it was something you wanted to learn more about. Is there anything holding you back from getting started, or are you ready to get set up?"

→ If yes: get their info, confirm partner, move to onboarding.

→ If hesitation: use objection responses below.

Objection Responses

1. "How much does it cost?"

"Nothing upfront — no monthly fee, no subscription. You only pay when I send you a lead, and it's $99 flat. You're not paying for visibility or a listing — you're paying for an actual homeowner who needs service right now. If a lead doesn't go anywhere, we talk about it."

2. "I already have enough work / we're pretty busy."

"That's the best position to be in, honestly. Most of my partners are busy too, which is why this works well: you only take leads you want. There's no obligation to accept every referral. When you're slow, you get more. When you're slammed, you can pause. I'd rather have you in the network for when things slow down than have you scrambling to fill your calendar then. Takes five minutes to set up."

3. "I've tried lead services before and they weren't good leads."

"Fair — and that's exactly why I built this differently. Homeowners fill out a form on phxpoolpro.com with their address, pool size, what they need, and their timeline. It's not scraped contact info — these are people who actively looked for help and submitted a request. I'm also keeping the partner network small for now, so you're not competing with 30 other companies for the same lead. You'd be one of very few in your area."

4. "We get most of our work from referrals — we don't need more leads."

"That's actually the profile of every strong partner I've brought on. This is just adding a consistent, qualified inbound channel on top of what you already do. Referral businesses slow down seasonally — this fills those gaps without you lifting a finger. And in Phoenix, homeowners are actively searching online. If you're not showing up there, someone else is."

5. "I need to think about it / talk to my partner."

"Completely understand. What's the main thing you'd want to think through? I'd rather answer that now and save you the back and forth. And if you do need to loop in your partner, I can send you a one-pager that covers exactly how it works so they have the same information I'm giving you."

→ Get a specific callback time before hanging up: "What's a good day for me to follow back up with you?"

Common Questions

1. "How does the matching work — what happens after I sign up?"

"A homeowner submits a request through our site — name, address, pool size, what they need, how urgent it is. I review it and match them to the right partner based on location and service type. You get an email with their details and contact info. You reach out, you close the job. That's it."

2. "What kind of jobs are these?"

"All types — repairs, maintenance, cleaning. The bulk right now is repairs and service calls. Phoenix summer is six months of something breaking — motors, pumps, heaters, leaks — homeowners are actively searching. As the network grows I can filter by job type and match better."

3. "How many leads would I get?"

"Honest answer: I'm still building the homeowner side, so I won't promise a number right now. What I can tell you is I'm running Google Ads to phxpoolpro.com once the network is set, and I'm keeping the partner count low — so each partner gets more, not less. You'd be getting in early."

4. "What if the lead doesn't answer or doesn't book?"

"That's a real concern and I want to handle it right. If a lead is unresponsive or the info is bad, we talk about it. I'm not running a volume game — my reputation depends on you actually closing jobs. I'd rather flag a bad lead than charge you for it."

5. "Is this legit?"

"PHX Pool Pro is a referral and matching service — I'm not a pool company, I'm a connector. Think of it like how Angi or Thumbtack works, but local and personal. I vet every homeowner request before it goes to a partner, and I vet every partner before they get leads. You're not paying for a listing — you're paying for a qualified referral."

6. "Do I have to sign a contract?"

"No contract, no commitment. You can stop anytime. The only agreement is a simple one-page partner confirmation — your contact info, preferred service areas, and payment preference. Nothing binding, nothing long-term. If it works, great. If it doesn't, no hard feelings."

Close

"Here's what I'd suggest — let me get you added as a partner today, no cost to do that. If the first lead comes in and it's not for you, just pass on it. But at least you're in the network and set up when summer hits and homeowners start flooding in. Does that work for you?"

Collect on close:

  • Full name and best contact number
  • Service area (zip codes or neighborhoods)
  • Services offered (repairs / maintenance / cleaning / all)
  • Preferred payment method for leads
