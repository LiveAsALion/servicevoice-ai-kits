Part 1: The Market

Problem Scale

  • Healthcare workers are ~10% of the U.S. workforce but absorb 48% of all nonfatal workplace violence injuries
  • Healthcare workers are 5x more likely to experience workplace violence than the average U.S. worker
  • WPV incidents increased 30% between 2021–2022
  • 75% of all U.S. workplace assaults occur in healthcare and social service settings (OSHA)
  • The American Hospital Association pegs total annual cost of WPV at $18.27 billion
  • ED nurses report assault rates as high as 12.7 per 100 workers per year
  • Reporting rates are estimated at 30–70% of actual incidents — cultural normalization is itself a correctable condition

Regulatory Tailwinds

Jurisdiction Mandate Status
California Cal/OSHA T8 §3342 + SB 553 (all employers, July 2024) Active — most comprehensive in nation
New York Hospital/nursing home WPV prevention law Signed Dec. 2025 — effective September 2026
Texas HB 2025 — violence prevention plans for healthcare Effective September 2024
Illinois, WA, OR, NJ, CT Active mandates or advanced legislation Rolling
Joint Commission New WPV prevention standards across behavioral health + home care July 2024 — auditable, documentation required

Federal OSHA rule is stalled (moved to Long-Term Action, September 2025). This actually helps: state-level mandates are accelerating to fill the void, creating a patchwork that makes a multi-state digital training product more valuable, not less.

New York’s September 2026 deadline is the single most urgent market activation in play right now.

The gap: No competitor leads with individual behavioral agency — the concept that the employee, through awareness, positioning, communication, and decision-making, is the first and most powerful layer of their own defense. Healthcare Guardian owns that positioning if it executes.

Part 2: Strategic Decisions Made

The 7 Questions — Final Answers

Q1 — Physical Content Scope Exclude from digital product entirely for launch. Reserve for in-person curriculum (future upsell). Simplifies legal review, removes friction for risk managers, keeps digital product clean for enterprise compliance sales.

Q2 — Avatar Identity Female presenter, named Morgan Hayes. AI avatar via HeyGen. Nursing workforce is 87% female; same-gender pedagogical agents produce higher engagement and perceived credibility for self-advocacy content. Elias Reed remains the curriculum architect and brand voice in written/email communications — Morgan is the on-screen expert voice. Clean distinction.

Q3 — Launch Scope Single pilot module first (Module 3: Pattern Recognition, ~10–12 minutes), then full curriculum build. Price: $29/seat, minimum 5 seats ($145). Goal of pilot: proof of purchase, qualitative feedback, completion rate data — not revenue.

Q4 — Business Entity Healthcare Guardian LLC — Arizona filing (~$50–85 via azcc.gov). EIN via IRS.gov (free, 5 minutes). Business bank account (Mercury recommended). Stripe under entity. This must happen before any money moves.

Part 5: Discovery Call Script

Use as a guide, not a script to read verbatim. Listen more than you talk.

Opening: “Thanks for connecting. I’ll keep this focused — I know your time is limited. I’ve been building Healthcare Guardian and I’m trying to make sure the program solves a real problem before I ask anyone to invest in it. I’d genuinely like to hear your perspective on what’s actually missing in your current training, if anything.”

Core Questions:

  1. “Walk me through what your current WPV training looks like — frequency, format, who delivers it.”
  2. “What does your staff tell you about it — does it stick? Do they feel better prepared after?”
  3. “If you could change one thing about how your organization approaches this, what would it be?”
  4. “Is this on your radar as a compliance issue right now, or a staff safety culture issue — or both?”
  5. “If I told you there was a program your staff could complete in under 90 minutes, that they’d actually retain, and that produces the documentation trail you need — what would that be worth annually?”

The Ask: “I’d like to offer you complimentary access to our pilot module — a complete 10-minute module on behavioral pattern recognition. You can assign it to a small group and give me honest feedback on whether it’s the right level for your team. No purchase required. Would that be useful?”

Strong Demand Signals: - Asks about pricing without being prompted - Mentions a specific incident or recent pattern - References a mandate or compliance deadline - Mentions budget authority

Flags and Risks

Geographic strategy deviation (Flag) The Phase 2 plan validated NY + CA as primary markets. The Arizona-focused outreach list (Banner Health, HonorHealth, Dignity Health, Carondelet) is a deviation. Arizona has no active WPV mandate. New York hospitals have a September 2026 deadline and are actively looking for solutions right now. The next outreach wave should correct this.

Email deliverability (Critical Blocker) Cold outreach from elias@healthcareguardiantraining.com is flagging as spam. Without domain authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC records), a properly warmed sending domain, and a clean IP, open rates will be below 10% regardless of copy quality. This must be fixed before any outreach is sent.

Physical content liability (Managed, not eliminated) Decision to exclude physical defense content from digital product is correct. Risk is not zero — even the behavioral content should have legal review before enterprise sales begin. Budget for this in Phase 3.

Validation vs. confirmation bias Positive survey responses are not paid transactions. The only hard validation signal is a paid purchase, even at $29. Separate soft interest from real demand in the tracking.

Discovery call exposure Elias Reed cannot be on a live voice call. The pilot demo flow describes a 20-minute discovery call but doesn’t specify who runs it. This gap needs a decision: use the self-serve flow only, hire a fractional SDR, or reconsider the real-identity vs. persona structure for call scenarios.
