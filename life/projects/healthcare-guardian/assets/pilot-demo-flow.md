# HCG Pilot Demo Flow — Module 3: Pattern Recognition
> Full guide for live demos and self-serve enrollment

## Purpose
- Guide for Elias to walk decision-makers through a live demo
- Blueprint for self-serve pilot enrollment end-to-end

**Who:** CNO, Risk Manager, or Education Director
**Setting:** 20-minute discovery call or in-person
**Goal:** Get them to say yes to a 5-seat pilot ($145)

The demo is NOT a product tour. It's a mirror — show them their problem, then show the solution.

---

## Phase 1: The Hook (2 min)

Open with: *"Before I show you anything — when's the last time one of your nurses told you they felt genuinely prepared to handle a patient escalation? Not just that they'd been through a training box, but actually prepared?"*

Follow with: *"That's exactly why we built this. Let me show you Module 3 — it's called Pattern Recognition, and it addresses the gap most facilities don't even know they have."*

---

## Phase 2: The Problem Frame (3 min)

Three stats from the one-pager:
- "75% of all workplace assaults happen in healthcare."
- "Only 30% of incidents get reported — the real number is at least 3x your OSHA 300 log."
- "The Joint Commission now requires a WPV prevention program. Not recommends — requires."

Then: *"Most organizations respond with a one-day in-service or an online course built for office workers. Your nurses know the difference."* [Pause. Let it land.]

---

## Phase 3: Live Module Demo (8 min)

Open Teachable pilot course on screen.

**Opening (1 min):** Play first 60-90 sec of Morgan Hayes intro.
*"This isn't a narrator reading slides. Morgan speaks like a senior clinician to a colleague."*

**Behavioral Escalation Sequence (3 min):**
- Walk through Color Code framework (White → Yellow → Orange → Red)
- Show clinical specificity: "What happens in an ED waiting room at 11pm Friday"
- Show vignette — pause on key moment: *"Right here — most staff freeze. They see behavior but have no language for it. This module gives them the framework to name it and respond before it peaks."*

**Knowledge Check (2 min):**
- Show one of 5 questions: *"It's a transfer exercise — practicing the decision, not recalling a definition."*
- Show answer rationale: *"If they got it wrong, they understand why — and won't make that mistake in the hallway."*

**Completion & Certificate (1 min):**
*"This generates a completion record automatically. That's your Joint Commission documentation and OSHA 300 log."*

**Pricing Close (1 min):**
*"The pilot is 5 seats at $29 — $145 total. One module. Your staff completes it this week. No contract. 12 minutes and a conversation."*

---

## Phase 4: Objection Handling (3 min)

**"We already have a WPV training program."**
"Good — this isn't a replacement, it's a layer. Most programs cover policy and reporting. This covers behavioral recognition and de-escalation. What does your current program teach staff to do in the 30 seconds before a situation peaks?"

**"Our staff don't have time for more training."**
"Module 3 is 12 minutes. Self-paced. They can do it between patients on a tablet. No scheduling, no classroom."

**"Our L&D team handles procurement."**
"I'd love to connect with them. Want me to send a one-pager they can review, or set up a 15-minute call together?"

**"What's the ROI?"**
"One nurse turnover event costs $40,000–$60,000. Structured de-escalation training reduces incidents 40–60%. The pilot is $145."

**"We need procurement / legal / IT."**
"The pilot bypasses that — it's a direct purchase, no vendor agreement, no IT integration. You can have 5 staff enrolled today and data to bring to procurement next week."

---

## Phase 5: The Close (2 min)

**If warm:** "I can send you the enrollment link right now. You forward it to 5 staff, they're in the course today. Want me to do that while we're on the call?"

**If needs time:** "I'll send the one-pager and enrollment link. Pilot pricing is good for 30 days. What's the best email for you?"

**Always get a specific next action.** Not "I'll think about it." A date, an email, a name.

---

## Signals to Watch For During Discovery Call
- Asks about pricing unprompted → Strong demand signal
- Mentions specific incident or pattern → Pain is real and recent
- References mandate or compliance deadline → External forcing function = faster buy
- Mentions budget authority → Short sales cycle potential

## Self-Serve Pilot Flow (No Demo)

1. **Landing page** → healthcareguardiantraining.com → "Start the Pilot" CTA
2. **Teachable enrollment** → "Healthcare Guardian Pilot: Pattern Recognition" → $29/seat, min 5 seats
3. **Automated confirmation email** with login credentials
4. **Staff complete module** on any device, self-paced
5. **Certificates generated** automatically on completion
6. **Admin follow-up email** (Day 3 post-completion) — feedback request + full curriculum offer

## Key Discovery Call Questions
- "Walk me through what your current WPV training looks like — frequency, format, who delivers it."
- "What does your staff tell you about it — does it stick?"
- "If you could change one thing about how your organization approaches this, what would it be?"
- "Is this on your radar as a compliance issue or a staff safety culture issue — or both?"
- "If I told you there was a program your staff could complete in under 90 minutes, that they'd actually retain, and that produces the documentation trail you need — what would that be worth annually?"
